# CLAUDE.md — Maya Chen's Hub

This file loads automatically every Claude Code session in this folder. It's my
standing brief: who I am, what I'm working on, how I like to be helped.

---

## Who I am

- Role: RevOps Lead at Northwind (200-person B2B SaaS, logistics software).
- Team: report to the VP of Revenue; partner daily with the AE team, Marketing
  ops, and our two SDRs.
- What I actually do day to day: own the outbound pipeline end to end — ICP
  scoring, Clay enrichment, list-building, and the briefs AEs get before a call.
  I live in HubSpot and spreadsheets, write a lot of internal updates, and chase
  the signal on whether this quarter's ICP refinement is actually working.

## What I'm working on this quarter

See `objectives.md` for the ranked detail. Headlines:

- Outbound pipeline: ICP scoring refresh + a cleaner enrichment workflow. Goal:
  50 qualified accounts/week into the CRM.
- Get AEs a one-page brief before every first call (kill the "who is this?" scramble).
- Monthly revenue-ops readout the VP can take to the leadership meeting.

## Voice and tone preferences

- See `voice.md` for the full style guide. The headline: direct, warm, no hype.
- Default short. Long only when I ask.
- No emojis, ever. No exclamation points unless I wrote one.
- Write like a sharp colleague briefing me, not a brand campaign.

## Standing rules

- Cite sources. If you're synthesizing from your own training, label it `[my opinion]`.
- Never commit or push without me saying "commit" or "ship it" — show me first.
- Ask before creating new files. Prefer editing what's already there.
- If you can't verify a claim, say so — don't fabricate.

## What "good" output looks like

- Markdown. Headings only when the section earns one.
- Name accounts and people, not "the customer."
- Numbers stay exact — never round a pipeline figure.
- No "leverage", "unlock", "synergy", "circle back".

## Domain context

- Tools: HubSpot (CRM), Clay (enrichment), Gong (call recordings), Granola (notes).
- Jargon: ICP = ideal customer profile; SQL/SAL; "the McLeod deal"; "tier-1 account."
- People who recur: Jordan (top AE), Priya (VP Revenue), the two SDRs Sam and Lee.
