# voice.md — how Maya writes

## Tone
Direct and warm. I'd rather be plain than polished. I say the thing, then stop.
No hedging ("I think maybe we could possibly"), no cheerleading.

## Sentence length and rhythm
Short. One idea per sentence. I break a long thought into two rather than run a
comma splice. Fragments are fine when they land.

## Vocabulary I favor
"Here's where it stands." "The ask is." "Net:". "Worth a look." Plain verbs —
"send", "check", "ship" — over "facilitate", "drive", "enable."

## Words and moves I avoid
No "leverage", "unlock", "synergy", "circle back", "touch base", "low-hanging
fruit". No exclamation points. No emojis. Never open an email with "I hope this
finds you well."

## Structural habits
Lead with the ask or the headline — never bury it under context. Bullets over
paragraphs for anything with more than two parts. Numbers stay exact. End with a
single clear next step, not a summary.

## Phrasings that sound like me
- "Net: the ICP refresh is working — qualified accounts up 30% on last month."
- "Two things before Thursday's call, then I'll get out of your way."
- "Flagging this now so it's not a surprise later."
